[h1]MapGen AI[/h1]
Describe your map in natural language — AI generates it for you.

Type anything like "mountain fortress with hot springs", "straight river on the left side", or "just surprise me" and watch the AI configure your map in real-time with Map Preview.

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]Natural Language Map Generation[/b] — Describe terrain in plain text, AI converts it to map parameters
[*] [b]Live Map Preview[/b] — See changes instantly through Map Preview integration
[*] [b]Elevation Shapes[/b] — Diagonal mountain ranges, central lakes, ring fortresses, canyons, slopes, and more
[*] [b]River Control[/b] — Direction, position, and straight river mode
[*] [b]Terrain Tuning[/b] — Rich soil density, vegetation, animals, ore, ruins, rock types, caves, geysers
[*] [b]Odyssey DLC Support[/b] — 60+ tile mutators (hot springs, fjords, oasis, animal habitats, etc.)
[*] [b]Preset System[/b] — Save and load your favorite map configurations
[*] [b]Iterative Refinement[/b] — Keep chatting to tweak your map until it's perfect
[*] [b]Korean / English[/b] — Full bilingual UI and AI responses
[/list]

[h2]Quick Start[/h2]
1. Subscribe to this mod and [url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2800857642]Map Preview[/url] (required)
2. Open [b]Mod Settings → MapGen AI[/b], select your LLM provider and enter your API key
3. On the world map, select a tile — click the [b]✦ AI Map Gen[/b] button next to Map Preview
4. Describe your ideal map and hit Send!

[h2]Tips[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]Describe everything in one message[/b] — For best results, put all your requirements in a single prompt rather than adding them one by one. For example, instead of "add mountains" → "now add a river" → "make it straight", try: "Mountains on the right with a straight river on the left". LLMs perform significantly better with single comprehensive instructions ([url=https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.06120]research shows up to 39% accuracy drop in multi-turn conversations[/url]).
[*] If you do need to tweak, the AI will try to preserve your previous settings — but a fresh single prompt is always more reliable.
[/list]
